Eat That Frog!: 21 Great Ways to Stop Procrastinating and Get More Done in Less Time

by Brian Tracy

There is never enough time to do everything you have to do. You are literally swamped with work and personal responsibilities, projects, stacks of magazines to read, and piles of books you intend to get to one of these days—as soon as you get caught up. - Tracy.

You can get control of your time and your life only by changing the way you think, work, and deal with the never-ending river of responsibilities that flows over you each day. I hope that this book changes your life forever. - Tracy.

Eat That Frog! provides the 21 most effective methods for conquering procrastination and accomplishing more. Although first published in 2001, it’s constantly revised and updated throughout, and includes brand new information on how to keep technology from dominating our time.

The first rule of frog eating is this: If you have to eat two frogs, eat the ugliest one first

Your frog is your biggest, most important task, and the one you are most likely to procrastinate on. It is also the one task that can have the greatest positive impact on your life and your results at the moment.

Here are the first steps to eating that frog!

Visualize Yourself as You Want to Be

Your mental picture of yourself has a powerful effect on your behaviour. Visualize yourself as the person you intend to be in the future. See yourself as the kind of person who gets important jobs done quickly and well on a consistent basis. Your self-image, the way you see yourself on the inside, largely determines your performance on the outside. All improvements in your outer life begin with improvements on the inside.
